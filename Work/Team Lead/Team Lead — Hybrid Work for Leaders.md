# Hybrid Work for Leaders

<!--
**Introduction**
This document is a guide for business leaders who want to make hybrid work work for their organizations. Hybrid work is a model that allows employees to work from different locations and at different times, depending on their preferences and needs. Hybrid work can offer many benefits, such as increased flexibility, wellbeing, productivity, and innovation. However, it also requires a deliberate, thoughtful, and strategic approach to address the challenges and opportunities of this new reality. This guide will share the insights and learnings from Microsoft's own experience of embracing hybrid work, and provide actionable steps and solutions for business leaders to get started today.



**Key Trends and Challenges**

The first section of the document presents five key trends and challenges that business leaders need to know in 2022, based on the findings from the annual Work Trend Index report. These are:

- Employees have a new "worth it" equation. They are rethinking what they want from their job and how it will impact their health, family, time, and purpose. They are also looking for more flexibility and wellbeing benefits from their employers.
- Managers feel wedged between leadership and employee expectations. They need more influence and resources to make changes on behalf of their team and to create team norms for the hybrid work experience.
- Leaders need to make the office worth the commute. They need to rethink the role of the physical space and design it for collaboration, connection, and innovation. They also need to adopt new cultural norms and technologies to ensure everyone feels included and engaged, regardless of where they are working from.
- Flexible work doesn't have to mean "always on". Leaders need to combat digital exhaustion and help employees achieve balance and focus. They need to use data and insights to understand work patterns and take steps to improve employee engagement and wellbeing.
- Rebuilding social capital looks different in a hybrid world. Leaders need to be intentional about reconnecting both hybrid and remote workers into the fabric of the organization. They need to foster a culture of continuous learning and offer technology that helps employees develop new skills and access institutional knowledge.



**Microsoft's Approach to Hybrid Work**

The second section of the document explains how Microsoft is preparing for the hybrid workplace across three key areas: culture, space, and technology. It also provides examples and best practices of how Microsoft is using its own solutions to transform its business processes and empower its employees. The three key areas are:

- Culture. Microsoft is prioritizing culture to enable hybrid work, by embracing flexibility as a principle, empowering managers to lead the shift, making Microsoft Viva the employee experience platform, rebuilding social capital, helping people learn and make an impact, focusing on culture to attract and retain talent, and combating digital exhaustion from the top.
- Space. Microsoft is designing for the people not in the room, by using Microsoft Teams Rooms to bring remote attendees "into the room" and make everyone feel like they have a seat at the table. Microsoft is also transforming its physical spaces into intelligent, cloud-powered services, by using Workplace Analytics, Azure Digital Twins, and Azure IoT to build the "brains" of its smart buildings. Microsoft is also preparing for the metaverse, by using Microsoft Mesh to enable presence and shared experiences from anywhere, on any device, through mixed reality applications.
- Technology. Microsoft is using technology to manage hybrid work at scale, by moving everything to the cloud, digitizing every business process, and mandating zero-trust security from cloud to edge. Microsoft is also using Teams to transform its business, by building custom apps, bots, and workflows directly within Teams. Microsoft is also working securely from anywhere with Windows 11 and Windows 365, by providing a secure and personalized Windows experience that can be accessed on any device.



**Actionable Steps to Get Started Today**

The third section of the document provides quick start guides for business leaders to get started with hybrid work today. The guides cover different functional areas, such as human resources, information technology and security, marketing and sales, real estate and facilities, and operations. The guides provide tips and tricks, best practices, and recommended solutions for each area. The document also provides a list of Microsoft solutions that are built for the era of hybrid work, enabling organizations to meet new employee and customer expectations, and gain competitive advantage.
