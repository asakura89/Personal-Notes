> from each skills you mentioned, expand sub-skills I need to learn

Sure, I can help you with that. Here are some sub-skills for each skill that I mentioned in the previous table:

- Project Management: This skill involves planning, executing, and monitoring projects to achieve specific goals and objectives. Some sub-skills for project management are:
    * Scope management: defining and controlling what is and is not included in the project
    * Time management: estimating, scheduling, and tracking the project activities and milestones
    * Cost management: estimating, budgeting, and controlling the project expenses and resources
    * Quality management: ensuring that the project deliverables meet the expectations and standards of the stakeholders
    * Risk management: identifying, analyzing, and mitigating the potential threats and uncertainties that may affect the project
    * Communication management: establishing and maintaining effective communication channels among the project team, stakeholders, and sponsors
    * Stakeholder management: identifying and managing the expectations and interests of the people who are involved in or affected by the project
    * Integration management: coordinating and aligning the various project elements and processes into a coherent whole
- Business Stakeholders: This skill involves understanding and engaging with the people who have a stake in the business, such as customers, employees, suppliers, investors, regulators, etc. Some sub-skills for business stakeholders are:
    * Stakeholder analysis: identifying and prioritizing the key stakeholders and their needs, expectations, and influence on the business
    * Stakeholder mapping: visualizing and categorizing the stakeholders according to their power, interest, and attitude towards the business
    * Stakeholder engagement: communicating and collaborating with the stakeholders to build trust, rapport, and support for the business objectives and initiatives
    * Stakeholder management: managing and balancing the conflicting or competing interests and expectations of the stakeholders
- Business Process: This skill involves designing, modeling, analyzing, and improving the workflows and activities that produce value for the business and its customers. Some sub-skills for business process are:
    * Business process modeling: creating and documenting the graphical representations of the current and future states of the business processes
    * Business process analysis: examining and evaluating the performance, efficiency, and effectiveness of the business processes
    * Business process improvement: identifying and implementing the changes and enhancements that can optimize the business processes and outcomes
    * Business process management: overseeing and controlling the execution and governance of the business processes
- Project Scope: This skill involves defining and managing the boundaries and deliverables of the project. Some sub-skills for project scope are:
    * Scope definition: specifying and documenting the features, functions, and requirements of the project
    * Scope verification: confirming and validating that the project scope meets the expectations and acceptance criteria of the stakeholders
    * Scope control: monitoring and managing the changes and variations that may affect the project scope
- Business Analysis: This skill involves eliciting, analyzing, and communicating the business requirements and solutions for a project or a problem. Some sub-skills for business analysis are:
    * Requirement elicitation: gathering and discovering the information and needs of the stakeholders and users
    * Requirement analysis: organizing, modeling, and validating the requirements and ensuring that they are clear, complete, consistent, and feasible
    * Requirement specification: documenting and communicating the requirements and solutions in a structured and understandable format
    * Requirement management: tracking and controlling the changes and updates to the requirements and solutions
- Visio: This skill involves using the Microsoft Visio software to create and edit diagrams, charts, and drawings that illustrate complex information and processes. Some sub-skills for Visio are:
    * Visio basics: understanding and navigating the Visio interface, tools, and features
    * Visio shapes: creating, formatting, and manipulating the graphical elements that represent the components of a diagram
    * Visio stencils: using and customizing the collections of shapes that are designed for specific diagram types and purposes
    * Visio templates: using and modifying the pre-defined layouts and settings that are available for different diagram categories and styles
    * Visio connectors: creating, editing, and arranging the lines and arrows that link the shapes and show the relationships and flows between them
    * Visio layers: organizing and managing the visibility and properties of the shapes and connectors in a diagram
    * Visio data: importing, exporting, and linking data from external sources to the shapes and connectors in a diagram
    * Visio collaboration: sharing and co-editing diagrams with other users and publishing diagrams to various formats and platforms
