# Taking Notes

## Introduction

Note-taking is a skill that can benefit not only students, but also working professionals. Whether you need to take notes during a meeting, a presentation, or a brainstorming session, having a good note-taking system and the right supplies can help you stay organized, productive, and efficient. In this essay, I will share with you some note-taking tips for working professionals and recommend some office-appropriate supplies that you can use. By following these tips, you will be able to take notes effectively, confidently, and professionally.



## Tip 1: Consider Your Note-Taking System

The first tip is to consider your note-taking system. The way you took notes as a student may not work for you as a professional. School notes are meant for information retention, whereas work notes are more action-oriented. We suggest establishing a consistent note-taking system that focuses on goals and tasks, rather than on details and facts. Additionally, as a professional, you want to choose office supplies that are reliable but also appropriate for work. You want to be taken seriously by your colleagues and superiors, so avoid using stationery that is too childish or flashy. Once you are familiar with your office's culture, you can decide if bringing in some themed stationery is acceptable or not.



## Tip 2: Color-Coding

Color-coding is a great way to draw attention to important tasks and deadlines. You can use different colors to highlight different types of information, such as due dates, urgent questions, action items, or key takeaways. We suggest using a multi-pen that has four ink colors and a mechanical pencil component, such as the Uni Jetstream 4&1 Metal Multi Pen. The Jetstream is a popular choice because of its low viscosity ink that writes smoothly and dries quickly. While a color-coding system can work in any notebook, we like the Leuchtturm1917 Hardcover Notebook that has an index for logging your meetings and notes. The graph and line versions also have a dedicated space at the top of each page for writing in the date.



## Tip 3: Diagramming

If you are a visual thinker, you may prefer diagramming over linear note-taking. Diagramming allows you to map out tasks, brainstorm concepts, and draw out your ideas in a more creative and intuitive way. You can use shapes, symbols, arrows, or colors to organize your notes visually. We suggest using a notebook that has plenty of space for mind mapping and regular notes alike, such as the Midori MD Frame Notebook Journal. It includes stickers for labeling the spine, small numbered index tabs for indicating different projects or important meetings, and a ribbon marker for keeping your place. Pair the notebook with a mechanical pencil that has an automatic lead advancement system and a protective lead sleeve, such as the Pentel Orenz Nero Mechanical Pencil. This pencil minimizes lead breakage and eliminates the need to click.



## Tip 4: Shorthand and Templating

To save time and space, you can create your own shorthand for commonly used words or phrases and store it in your notebook on repositionable sticky notes. These sticky notes can be moved from one meeting to the next without damaging the paper underneath. They can also be used as page flags for your current notes or older ones you need to reference. We recommend using the Hightide Sticky Tabs that come in various colors and sizes. You can also follow a general format that is easy to remember and provides space for all the pertinent information. The A5 Special Memo Maruman Mnemosyne Notebook features two bolder lines that divide the page into three main sections, making it easy to organize meeting notes. Here is our template that you can adapt to your preferences:
- At the top, fill in the date and title of the meeting.
- Write down the names of the participants in case you have a question later and need to speak with them.
- Use the first section for the meeting agenda, open items or any questions you would like addressed.
- Allocate the second section for key points and the third section for next steps and to-dos for yourself or your team.

Those who prefer having a pre-made format can try the Laconic Style Notebook that covers 30 meetings, each with a two-page spread.



## Tip 5: Make Your Notes Actionable

After a meeting, review your notes and start planning out next steps. Here are some tips for what you can do after and in between meetings:
- Get the information you need before starting tasks. If you have a question or need clarification on a talking point during a meeting, make a note and broach it when it is time for discussion. If you are not able to ask your question, be sure to speak to the relevant team member as soon as possible while everything is fresh in your mind.
- At the end of a meeting, take time to confirm action items. This ensures that everyone is on the same page and knows what they need to do next.
- Prioritize your action items. Right after a meeting, transfer the action items you have accumulated to a memo pad that you can place on your desk. This will keep you focused on your tasks and remind you of your deadlines. We suggest using the Midori Stand Memo Pad that has different formats, such as to-do lists, weekly schedules, and hourly schedules.
- Set mini goals. Completing small, achievable goals helps build momentum for accomplishing bigger goals later. Keep track of your progress using a whiteboard notebook that has plastic sheets that protect your writing and also act as another writing surface. You can erase the writing on the plastic sheet or whiteboard depending on how your goals change. We recommend using the Gakken Write White+ Whiteboard Notebook that includes two blank pages, three graph pages and one to-do page with lines for 36 tasks.

Note-taking is a skill that can benefit not only students, but also working professionals. Whether you need to take notes during a meeting, a presentation, or a brainstorming session, having a good note-taking system and the right supplies can help you stay organized, productive, and efficient. In this essay, we shared with you some note-taking tips for working professionals and recommended some office-appropriate supplies that you can use. By following these tips, you will be able to take notes effectively, confidently, and professionally.



## Key Takeaways

- The five note-taking tips for working professionals are:
- Consider your note-taking system and choose office-appropriate supplies.
- Color-code your notes to highlight important information.
- Diagram your notes to visualize your ideas and concepts.
- Use shorthand and templating to save time and space.
- Make your notes actionable by reviewing them and planning out next steps.
- These tips will help you take notes effectively, confidently, and professionally.
