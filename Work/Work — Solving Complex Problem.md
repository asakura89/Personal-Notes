Steps in Differential Diagnosis
Differential diagnosis is a step-by-step process that doctors use to solve medical mysteries:
 * Gather Information: The first step is like collecting evidence at a crime scene. The doctor asks the patient detailed questions about their symptoms, when they started, and how they've changed. They also review the patient's medical history and medications. A physical examination provides additional clues.
 * Brainstorm Possible Causes: Based on the gathered information, the doctor creates a list of all possible illnesses that could explain the patient's condition. This is like the detective creating a list of suspects.
 * Narrow Down the List: Not all the suspects on the initial list are equally likely. The doctor considers factors like how common certain illnesses are, how well they fit the patient's symptoms and medical history, and the results of any initial tests. This helps narrow down the list of possibilities.
 * Refine the Diagnosis: The doctor may recommend additional tests to gather more specific clues and rule out certain illnesses on the list. As they get more information, the most likely diagnosis becomes clearer.
 * Reach a Conclusion: Once the doctor has enough evidence (test results), they reach a conclusion about the most likely cause of the patient's condition. This is the final diagnosis, which helps determine the best course of treatment.
