# Results and Outcomes

<!--

The difference between results and outcomes is not very clear, and sometimes they can be used interchangeably. However, according to some sources, there are some subtle distinctions between them:
- Results are usually connected to the end of a process, while outcomes are less controlled and a bit more unexpected[^1].
- Results refer to the conclusion or end to which any course or condition of things leads, or which is obtained by any process or operation; consequence or effect. Outcomes refer to the information, event, object or state of being produced as a result or consequence of a plan, process, accident, effort or other similar action or occurrence[^2].
- Results are systematic (or analytic), and thus there is a discernible/predictable correlation between cause and effect. Outcomes are systemic, which lacks a discernible/predictable correlation between cause and effect[^1].
- Outcomes are what the business wants or needs to achieve. Outputs are the actions or items that contribute to achieving an outcome. Outcomes are the results, and outputs are the activities that support the desired results[^3].

Here are some examples of how to use results and outcomes in sentences:
- The match result was a 1-0 win for the home team. The match outcome was a surprise for many fans.
- What was the result of your conversation with her? What was the outcome of your meeting with him?
- The project team delivered excellent results, but the outcomes were not as expected due to external factors.
- The outcomes of this course are outlined in your syllabus. The results of your assessment will be available next week.
