# #1 — Things to consider

Online services do store data differently. There are those who store temporarily in the browser. There are those who store data temporarily on their web server. There are also who store data permanently which require account deletion request procedure to be able to ask them to remove our data entirely from their possession.

Even if we have control over data that these services store, like the in-browser data storage. We as human also have human traits such as forgetful. One day you forget about services that still storing your data inside browser, then the next day they change their Terms of Service and decide to store the data on their server. The in-browser data moved to their server without your knowledge but with your consent because you skipped to read the whole new updated Terms of Service. Because you thought that, oh they update some feature. Then you lose the control over data you think still in your control. 

There are services that are not saving or storing your data

chatGPT, github copilot, bing AI copilot
