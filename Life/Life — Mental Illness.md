**Mental Illnesses**

Mental illnesses are health conditions that involve changes in emotion, thinking, or behavior. These changes can cause distress and/or problems functioning in social, work, or family activities⁸. Mental illnesses are common and nothing to be ashamed of. They are medical problems, just like heart disease or diabetes⁸.

Mental illnesses can take many forms. Some are mild and only interfere in limited ways with daily life, such as certain phobias. Other mental health conditions are so severe that a person may need care in a hospital⁸. Mental illnesses can affect anyone regardless of age, gender, geography, income, social status, race, ethnicity, religion/spirituality, sexual orientation, background, or other aspect of cultural identity⁸.

Mental illnesses are treatable and improvement is possible. Many people with mental health conditions return to full functioning⁸. Mental health is the foundation for emotions, thinking, communication, learning, resilience, hope, and self-esteem. Mental health is also key to relationships, personal and emotional well-being, and contributing to community or society⁸.

**Grounding Techniques**

Grounding techniques are exercises that help you refocus on the present moment to distract yourself from anxious feelings². Grounding is a practice that can help you pull away from flashbacks, unwanted memories, and negative or challenging emotions². These techniques can help create space from distressing feelings in nearly any situation².

Grounding techniques can be categorized into three types: physical, mental, and soothing². Physical grounding techniques use your five senses or tangible objects to help you move through distress². Mental grounding techniques involve focusing your mind⁵. Soothing grounding techniques involve calming your mind and body².

**Possible Grounding Techniques**

Here are some grounding techniques you can try:

1. **Put your hands in water**: Focus on the water’s temperature and how it feels on your fingertips, palms, and the backs of your hands².
2. **Pick up or touch items near you**: Focus on the texture and color of each item².
3. **Breathe deeply**: Slowly inhale, then exhale. Feel each breath filling your lungs and note how it feels to push it back out².
4. **Savor a food or drink**: Take small bites or sips of a food or beverage you enjoy, letting yourself fully taste each bite².
5. **Take a short walk**: Concentrate on your steps. Notice the rhythm of your footsteps and how it feels to put your foot on the ground and then lift it again².
6. **Hold a piece of ice**: What does it feel like at first? How long does it take to start melting? How does the sensation change when the ice begins to melt?²
7. **Savor a scent**: Is there a fragrance that appeals to you? Inhale the fragrance slowly and deeply and try to note its qualities².
8. **Move your body**: Do a few exercises or stretches. Pay attention to how your body feels with each movement and when your hands or feet touch the floor or move through the air².
9. **Listen to your surroundings**: Take a few moments to listen to the noises around you².

Remember, it's important to find what works best for you. If one technique doesn't work, don't be discouraged. Try another one. It may take some time and experimentation to find what helps you the most. If you're dealing with a mental health condition, it's also important to seek professional help. Grounding techniques are just one part of a comprehensive treatment plan.

**Crying During Sleep: How is it Possible?**

Crying during sleep is a phenomenon that can occur in both infants and adults³. It's important to understand that emotions, senses, and some muscle movements are far from inactive, even during deep sleep⁴. This means that emotional processes like crying can still happen, even without your conscious realization⁴. You might not remember the dream or the reason for crying because not all dreams are remembered, especially if you wake up during the non-Rapid Eye Movement (non-REM) stage of your sleep cycle¹.

**Reasons or Causes of Crying During Sleep**

There are a mix of emotional triggers and physical factors that can cause you to cry in your sleep⁴. Here are some possible reasons:

1. **Stress and Anxiety**: Mental health conditions like depression or Generalized Anxiety Disorder (GAD) can cause crying during sleep⁴.
2. **Unresolved Emotions, Trauma, or Grief**: Past trauma or grief can manifest in our sleep due to our brains performing daily processing functions⁴.
3. **Night Terrors and Nightmares**: These can cause crying in sleep and upon waking¹.
4. **Sleep Stage Transitions**: Babies cry in their sleep because they are not used to transitioning from deep sleep to light sleep¹.
5. **Physical Conditions**: Conditions like allergies, conjunctivitis, or a blocked tear duct can cause watering eyes¹.

**Is it a Bad Thing?**

Crying in your sleep can sometimes lead to potential disruptions in sleep quality that could have knock-on effects on your overall mental wellbeing⁴. Persistent sleep crying could be a sign of worsening depression⁸. However, releasing stress from the body and mind by crying can make us sleepy, especially as it releases endorphins and oxytocin⁷. These are self-soothing hormones that improve mood and relax us⁷. So, it's not necessarily a bad thing, but if it happens frequently and affects your sleep quality, it's advisable to seek professional help.

**How to Prevent Crying During Sleep**

Preventing crying during sleep involves a holistic approach that includes self-help strategies, lifestyle modifications, and sometimes professional intervention⁴. Here are some tips:

1. **Engage in calming activities**: Like reading or gentle stretching before bedtime⁴.
2. **Meditation**: It can help to calm the mind as you begin to fall asleep⁹.
3. **Maintain a regular sleep schedule**: This can help regulate your body's natural sleep-wake cycle⁴.
4. **Seek professional help**: If crying in sleep happens too frequently, consider seeking help from a mental health professional⁴.

Remember, it's important to listen to your body and mind. If you're dealing with a mental health condition, it's also important to seek professional help. Crying during sleep is just one part of a comprehensive treatment plan.

**Consistent Nightmares and Mental Illness**

Nightmares are intense dreams that can provoke terror, anger, or disgust³. Chronic nightmares have been associated with mental health conditions such as depression, schizophrenia, post-traumatic stress disorder (PTSD), and some personality disorders⁴⁹. Nightmares are also frequently present with mental health disorders including PTSD, insomnia, schizophrenia, psychosis, and mood, anxiety, adjustment, and personality disorders[^10^]. Therefore, consistent nightmares could be a sign of a mental illness.

**Nightmares Triggering Crying During Sleep**

Nightmares can cause intense emotional reactions, which might lead to crying during sleep³. Night terrors, which are episodes where you partially awake from slow-wave sleep, generally involve signs of extreme stress, like screaming or waving your limbs³. You probably won’t remember them, but a broken lamp or a concerned roommate may clue you in to what happened³. Therefore, it's possible that nightmares could trigger crying during sleep.

**Work Burnout and Mental Illness**

Burnout is a state of physical, emotional, and mental exhaustion¹⁴. It's often the result of chronic and poorly managed workplace stress¹⁷. People suffering from work burnout can be at risk for anxiety, depression, distress, poor decision-making, shortened attention span, and lack of motivation¹⁶. Therefore, burnout from work could potentially lead to mental illness.

**Work Burnout and Crying During Sleep**

Burnout results from excessive and prolonged emotional, physical, and mental stress²¹. This stress can make you tense, anxious, and cause sleep problems or worsen existing ones²¹. The stress and anxiety you are feeling can manifest as sleep-crying as your brain is working out the overwhelming tension that is going on in your life²³. Therefore, it's possible that burnout from work could lead to crying during sleep.

**Connections Between Mental Illness, Crying During Sleep, Having No Relationship, and Nightmares**

Mental illness, crying during sleep, having no relationship, and nightmares are all interconnected. Mental illnesses can cause nightmares and crying during sleep. For example, stress and trauma from mental illnesses can cause nightmares¹². Similarly, mental health issues can lead to changes in sleep habits or patterns, which can negatively affect health¹¹. These changes can include crying during sleep¹¹.

Having no relationship can also contribute to mental illness and crying during sleep. Relationships provide emotional support, which can help manage stress and anxiety.
