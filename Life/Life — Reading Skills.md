# Reading Skills

**Predicting**
Predicting is a reading strategy that involves making educated guesses about what will happen next in a text. This skill is important because it helps readers engage with the text and understand it more deeply. Predicting involves using information from the text and your own knowledge to guess what might happen next. For example, if a character in a story is described as sneaky and dishonest, you might predict that this character will betray someone later in the story. Predicting helps you stay engaged with the text and makes reading more enjoyable.

**Skimming**
Skimming is a method of quickly looking over a text to get a general idea of its content without reading every word. When you skim a text, you might look at the headings, subheadings, and any highlighted or bolded words. You might also read the first and last sentence of each paragraph. Skimming is useful when you're trying to decide whether a text is worth reading in detail. For example, you might skim a newspaper article to see if it's about a topic you're interested in.

**Scanning**
Scanning is a reading technique used when you're looking for specific information in a text. Unlike skimming, which is about getting a general idea of a text, scanning involves looking for particular words or phrases. For example, if you're reading a textbook and you want to find information about a specific topic, you might scan the text for keywords related to that topic. Scanning is a useful skill for research and studying.

**Recognizing Links**
Recognizing links is about understanding how different parts of a text are connected. This might involve recognizing how different ideas in a text relate to each other, or how the text connects to other texts you've read. Recognizing links can also involve understanding how the text connects to your own experiences and knowledge. This skill is important for understanding the deeper meanings in a text and for making connections between different texts.

Each of these skills plays a crucial role in reading and understanding texts. By practicing these skills, you can become a more effective and efficient reader. Remember, like any skill, these reading skills take time and practice to develop. So, keep reading and practicing!
