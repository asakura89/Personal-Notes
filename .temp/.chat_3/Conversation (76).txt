## Bing
A RACI matrix is a type of responsibility assignment matrix (RAM) used in project management. It is a simple spreadsheet or table that lists all stakeholders on a project and clarifies their roles and responsibilities. The acronym RACI stands for the different responsibility types: Responsible, Accountable, Consulted, and Informed.
- Responsible: The individual(s) with responsibility for the task or deliverable is typically responsible for developing and completing the project deliverables themselves. The responsible parties are typically hands-on team members that make direct contributions toward the completion of the project.
- Accountable: Accountable parties ensure accountability to project deadlines, and ultimately, accountability to project completion. This group frequently also falls under the informed category.
- Consulted: Consulted individuals’ opinions are crucial, and their feedback needs to be considered at every step of the game. These individuals provide guidance that is often a prerequisite to other project tasks.
- Informed: Informed individuals are kept up-to-date on progress, often only on completion of the task or deliverable, and with whom there is just one-way communication.
A RACI matrix can help project managers keep track of team roles and relay those responsibilities to the larger team. It defines clear roles and responsibilities for individual team members across the various phases of the project, breaking each role down into four types of designation: those who are Responsible and Accountable for project deliverables, those who should be Consulted as work begins, and stakeholders who need to be Informed of ongoing progress, roadblocks, and updates. what are differences between Responsible and Accountable
## Bing
The main difference between Responsible and Accountable is that the person who is Responsible is the one who actually performs the task or deliverable, while the person who is Accountable is the one who ensures that the task or deliverable is completed on time and to the required standard .
In other words, the person who is Responsible is typically a hands-on team member who makes direct contributions to the completion of the project, while the person who is Accountable ensures that deadlines are met and that the project is completed. It's important to note that while there can be multiple people who are Responsible for a task or deliverable, there should only be one person who is Accountable for it.
